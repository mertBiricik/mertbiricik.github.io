From OSINT Insights to AI-based Protection: Enhancing Cybersecurity Resilience in Power Distribution Systems

M. I. Ecevit, M. Biricik, T. A. Uğurlu, A. Özdemir, O. Ceylan, H. Dağ, C. Rodio, R. Lazzari

Proceedings of the 60th International Universities Power Engineering Conference (UPEC), IEEE, 2025

Abstract

This research presents a comprehensive approach to enhancing cybersecurity resilience in power distribution systems through the integration of Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) methodologies and artificial intelligence-based protection mechanisms. The study addresses critical vulnerabilities in modern power infrastructure and proposes novel AI-driven solutions for real-time threat detection and response.

Key Contributions

  • Development of OSINT-based threat intelligence gathering framework
  • Implementation of AI-driven anomaly detection algorithms for power systems
  • Integration of machine learning models for predictive cybersecurity analysis
  • Evaluation of protection mechanisms in critical infrastructure environments

Customer-Level Aggregation: The Imperative for Reliable Non-Technical Loss Detection Amidst Row-Level Analysis Pitfalls

M. Küçük, H. Coşkun, M. Biricik, B. E. Bülbül

Proceedings of the 60th International Universities Power Engineering Conference (UPEC), IEEE, 2025

Abstract

This paper addresses critical challenges in non-technical loss detection within power distribution systems by proposing customer-level aggregation methods. The research demonstrates significant improvements in detection accuracy while avoiding common pitfalls associated with row-level analysis approaches in electricity theft identification.

Key Contributions

  • Novel customer-level aggregation methodology for electricity theft detection
  • Comprehensive analysis of row-level analysis limitations and biases
  • Development of robust machine learning pipeline for anomaly detection
  • Empirical validation using real-world power distribution data
